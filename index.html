Overview

Astitva is the flagship scholarly series of the Global Synergetic Foundation (GSFN), an independent interdisciplinary research and publishing initiative devoted to the advancement of rigorous inquiry across diverse domains of knowledge. Founded in 2015, the series encourages sustained engagement between classical knowledge traditions and the questions that animate contemporary scientific thought.

Its central concern lies in the exploration of fundamental questions relating to knowledge, causality, consciousness, and the structure of reality. By bringing distinct traditions of inquiry into dialogue, the series seeks to illuminate both their internal coherence and their wider intellectual significance.

Intellectual Origins

The Astitva Series arises from a longer intellectual undertaking. Much of the work presented here has grown from more than two decades of sustained svādhyāya—disciplined study, reflection, and close engagement with foundational texts, particularly the Veda and the wider traditions of Indian philosophical thought.

Over this extended period of inquiry, reflections, working notes, and exploratory writings accumulated in the course of an ongoing effort to examine questions surrounding astitva—the nature of being and existence. The present series represents the more mature expression of those investigations.

The Svādhyāya Archive

Rather than allowing these earlier explorations to disappear behind finished publications, they have been preserved in a companion scholarly archive titled Astitva: Svādhyāya-Saṁhitā.

The archive records the gradual unfolding of ideas that eventually reach a more settled form in completed works. It is presented in the spirit of intellectual transparency and serves as a documentary record of the research process through which scholarly inquiry develops over time.

Scope of Research

The Astitva Series is intentionally multidisciplinary. It welcomes research exploring the intersections of Vedic and Indian knowledge systems with contemporary fields including:

  • Philosophy and foundations of knowledge
  • Mathematics and formal reasoning
  • Theoretical physics and cosmology
  • Cognitive and brain sciences
  • Complex systems and knowledge science
  • Integrative medicine and Ayurveda

Through such dialogue the series seeks to deepen understanding of the conceptual structures underlying both classical traditions of thought and modern scientific inquiry.

Publications

Publications in the series include research monographs, advanced scholarly studies, critical editions, annotated translations, articles, and works of digital scholarship. Each contribution is expected to maintain high standards of intellectual rigour, careful documentation, and openness to dialogue across disciplines and traditions.

Continuing Development

Now in its second decade, the Astitva Series continues to develop its digital and editorial infrastructure while remaining faithful to the purpose with which it began: the sustained exploration of knowledge traditions that take the fundamental questions of existence as their proper subject.

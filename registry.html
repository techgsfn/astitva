Astitva Archive Registry

The Astitva Archive serves as the institutional registry for scholarly publications issued within the research programme of the Global Synergetic Foundation (GSFN).

The archive provides a stable archival and citation framework for monographs, micro-monographs, and research communications exploring the foundations of existence, cognition, and knowledge systems.

The Three Research Series

The archive is organised into three interconnected publication series, each representing a distinct dimension of an ongoing philosophical and scientific investigation.

Series Conceptual Orientation
Astitva Foundational investigations into existence, consciousness, and the philosophical structures underlying knowledge systems.
Vedic-Hṛdaya-Cakṣu Studies exploring the interpretive vision through which Vedic knowledge systems may be understood in their deeper philosophical and scientific dimensions.
Svagarbhā Advanced research on the inner generative principles of Vedic thought, including Agni, cosmology, cognition, and knowledge structures.

The conceptual trajectory of the research programme may be expressed as:

Astitva → Vedic-Hṛdaya-Cakṣu → Svagarbhā → Astitva

This cyclic structure reflects the methodological orientation of the research programme in which the investigation of knowledge systems ultimately returns to the fundamental question of existence itself.

Publication Identifiers

Each publication issued within the archive receives a stable AST Series Identifier. This identifier functions as an internal archival reference used for cataloguing, citation, and long-term metadata management.

Individual publications are documented through dedicated landing pages which contain the canonical metadata records associated with each work, including identifiers such as ISBN or DOI when assigned.

The evolving catalogue of publications within the archive is maintained separately in the Astitva Archive Catalogue.
